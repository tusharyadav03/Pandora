Wireframes & Design Notes

A kind, fast, browser-only scanner for the 80% of devs who are not security specialists. This pack documents the user flow, key screens, design tokens, and interaction rationale behind the hackathon MVP.

v1.0 · 14 APR 2026
DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Four rules we refused to break.

When something broke, we asked which principle we violated — and worked back from there.

1. Kindness over cleverness

Every finding must read like a helpful teammate, not a compliance PDF.

2. Three-second paths

Paste → scan → understand → fix. No login, no onboarding, no settings.

3. Anxiety, then relief

Risk meter creates urgency; Beginner Mode and fixes remove it.

4. Demo-first polish

Optimize for the 30 seconds a hackathon judge actually watches.

WIREFRAME 04

Beginner Mode toggled.

Same findings, translated. Tone shift is the entire value prop.

AI Explanation 🧒 Beginner Mode
Imagine your config file is a sticky note on your front door. Right now it has your house key, your car key, and your debit card PIN written on it. Anyone who walks past can read it. We're going to move the keys to a locked drawer (an environment variable), and then you'll throw away the old keys and cut new ones — just in case someone already saw them.
6
Tone is a product feature. The Beginner voice is a prompt we author, version, and test like any other asset.

We shipped these, not aspired to them.

Contrast ≥ AA

All text passes WCAG AA against its background — including the coral on white pill.

Keyboard-only

Every action (scan, upload, toggle, copy) is reachable with Tab / Enter only.

Empty input

Scan button is disabled until textarea has non-whitespace content.

Oversize file

Files over 100K chars are rejected client-side with an explanatory toast.

Offline / API fail

Falls back to regex-only mode. Banner explains AI explanation is unavailable.

Mobile

Two-column results collapse to stacked cards under 860 px.
